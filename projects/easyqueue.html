Pop up web app for students and TAs

Designed for the CLUE study center at University of Washington

Easy to queue
For CLUE study center

This pop up application is designed for students and TAs in University of Washington CLUE study center.

It is hard for TAs to find the students

CLUE study centered is located at Mary Gates Hall Common of University of Washington, operating from 7 p.m. to midnight, Sunday to Thursday in general academic quarters.

For students, They need to find the table of the corresponding subjects, though it might already be really crowded, and they have to be at another table, which makes it even harder for TA to find them.

For teaching assistants, TAs read the names on the white board that students put on, then shout out the name to find this student, which causes a lot of noise and distract others.

Echos in the common
cause distractions

We conducted the user research of observation. We specifically paid mroe attention to tables that are extremely crowded, and normally they are popular subjects like maths, physics, chemistry and computer science.

Normally students who need help in these subjects cannot find good seats because of limited seating. So TAs have a harder time finding them.

Project In Process

This project is still being working on right now. Most of the results, including the work flow, user interfaces and others, are not definite yet. I will upload the final deliverable and process blog once it is finished.
